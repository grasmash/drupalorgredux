# Drupal Enterprise Proof Points & Case Studies

Research compiled March 2026. All claims include sources. Where numbers are approximate or dated, this is noted.

## Individual Organization Case Studies

### NASA

- **On Drupal since:** ~2013-2014 (Dries Buytaert blogged about Tesla on Drupal in 2011; NASA's Drupal migration was completed and won its first Webby in 2014)
- **Scale:**
  - 250,000+ pages migrated
  - ~3 terabytes of content
  - ~1 million average daily page views
  - Over 1 billion visits annually
  - ~500 content editors performing 2,000+ content updates per day
  - 30+ Drupal applications across 7 NASA centers
  - 50+ Drupal websites managed by Mobomo
- **Peak performance:** 40 million page views in a single day and 2 million+ concurrent users during the 2017 Total Solar Eclipse
- **Migration speed:** Migrated and relaunched www.nasa.gov and all subdomain components in just 13 weeks
- **Awards:** People's Voice Webby Award for Excellence in 2014, 2016, 2017, and 2018
- **User satisfaction:** First-time visitor satisfaction increased from 77% to 81%; 80% strongly agreed new design was pleasing
- **Architecture:** Headless Drupal on AWS, deployed across multiple availability zones
- **Note:** NASA's flagship site (nasa.gov) was later migrated to WordPress. science.nasa.gov and other properties continued on Drupal.
- **Sources:**
  - https://new.drupal.org/case-study/nasagov
  - https://www.mobomo.com/portfolio/nasa-website/
  - https://www.mobomo.com/2015/06/nasa-gov-uses-headless-drupal-reach-new-heights/

### Tesla

- **On Drupal since:** At least 2011 (Dries Buytaert blogged about it February 8, 2011)
- **Fortune 500 rank (2018):** #260
- **Implementation:** tesla.com runs on Drupal (also reported alongside Adobe Experience Manager); supports 30+ regional/language variants
- **Scale:** Handles traffic surges during major product launches (Cybertruck, Model 3 trims) without performance degradation
- **Capabilities:** Rich multimedia, interactive product pages, e-commerce transactional support, global multi-language
- **Sources:**
  - https://dri.es/tesla-motors-using-drupal (Feb 2011)
  - https://www.drupal.org/tesla-motors

### The Economist

- **On Drupal since:** ~2010 (migration documented October 2010, DrupalCon London session 2011)
- **Migrated from:** Custom Cold Fusion application + Oracle database
- **Scale:** 20+ million monthly readers worldwide; 20-30 million page views/month at time of migration
- **Contribution to Drupal:** The Economist sponsored the open-sourcing of the Migrate module (now a core part of Drupal's migration framework), developed by Cyrve for their data migration
- **Implementations:** economist.com (homepage, articles, channels, comments), 1843 Magazine (formerly Intelligent Life)
- **Sources:**
  - https://new.drupal.org/case-study/digital-transformation-of-the-economist-intelligent-life-to-1843-magazine
  - https://www.drupal.org/forum/general/show-off-your-drupal-site/2010-10-01/the-economistcom-data-migration-to-drupal

### Pfizer

- **On Drupal since:** At least 2013 (DrupalCon Prague 2013: "The Pfizer Story: Building a Global Digital Marketing Platform with Drupal")
- **Scale:**
  - ~500 Drupal websites in production
  - ~200 migrations per year from non-Drupal to Drupal
  - PfizerPro homepage + all 60 microsites migrated to Drupal
  - Digital marketing sites across 46 countries
- **Strategy:** Standardized ALL company web properties on Drupal; created the "Drupal Cookbook" for best practices
- **Open source mandate:** Pfizer requires all agency partners to contribute code back to Drupal. "Pfizer only works with agencies and vendors that contribute back to Drupal." Pfizer contributed directly to Drupal 8's Workflow Initiative.
- **Quote:** "Pfizer doesn't fork Drupal modules... It's all about security."
- **Sources:**
  - https://dri.es/why-large-organizations-are-choosing-to-contribute-to-drupal
  - https://prague2013.drupal.org/session/pfizer-story-building-global-digital-marketing-platform-drupal.html
  - https://www.appnovation.com/our-work/pfizer-business-transformation-to-deliver-consistency-quality-efficiency
  - https://www.appnovation.com/portfolio/pfizerpro-drupal-migration

## Enterprise Proof Points

### Fortune 500 Adoption

- **47 Fortune 500 companies** were identified using Drupal for their websites in 2018 (up from 23 in 2014 and 33 earlier in 2018 by a different count)
- Notable Fortune 500 Drupal users include: CVS Health (#7), General Electric (#18), Pfizer (#57), Tesla (#260), General Dynamics (#99), Raytheon (#119), Qualcomm (#133), Cummins (#149), Danaher (#162), Stanley Black & Decker (#228), Xerox (#291), KKR (#470), Regeneron (#473), and many more
- **Source:** https://www.zyxware.com/articles/5918/list-of-fortune-500-companies-using-drupal-for-their-websites-2018

### Gartner & Forrester Recognition

- **Acquia** (Drupal's primary commercial platform provider) has been named a **Leader in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Digital Experience Platforms (DXP) for six consecutive years** (2020-2025)
- In 2025, Acquia is joined in the Leader quadrant by Optimizely and Adobe
- Acquia is **the only open-source provider** to be positioned as a Leader in both the Gartner Magic Quadrant and the Forrester Wave for Web Content Management / DXP
- Acquia was also a Leader in the 2017 Forrester Wave for Web Content Management Systems
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.acquia.com/resources/report/acquia-named-leader-2025-gartner-magic-quadranttm-dxp
  - https://dri.es/acquia-a-leader-in-the-2024-gartner-magic-quadrant-for-digital-experience-platforms
  - https://dri.es/acquia-a-leader-in-the-2017-forrester-wave-for-web-content-management-systems

### Government Adoption

- **WhiteHouse.gov** ran on Drupal from 2009 to 2017 (Obama administration)
- **150+ US federal government sites** powered by Drupal, including sites for the departments of Education, Energy, Commerce, Health, Defense, Justice, Transportation, Homeland Security, and Agriculture
- Additional government users: House of Representatives, NASA, IRS, USPS
- **34 US state and territory agencies** use Drupal
- **~56% of the world's government websites** used Drupal as of 2021
- Countries with central government portals on Drupal include: Australia, Estonia, France, Germany, India, and South Africa
- Among large US state government sites, Drupal holds **100% of the open-source CMS market share**
- Among large US city government sites, Drupal holds **50% of the open-source CMS market share**
- **Sources:**
  - https://dri.es/whitehouse-gov-using-drupal
  - https://evolvingweb.com/blog/drupal-government-websites-6-reasons-why-its-best-option
  - https://www.thedroptimes.com/41336/drupal-usage-in-government-data-driven-study-cms-adoption-patterns
  - https://www.govtech.com/archive/why-big-sites-run-drupal.html

### Higher Education Adoption

- **71-80% of the top 100 universities worldwide** use Drupal (sources vary: 71% per one study, 80% per another)
- **All 8 Ivy League universities** use Drupal:
  - Harvard University: 50+ Drupal sites
  - Cornell University: 140+ Drupal sites
  - Brown University: 70+ Drupal sites
  - University of Pennsylvania: 60+ Drupal sites
  - Columbia University: 50+ Drupal sites
  - Princeton University: 35+ Drupal sites
  - Yale University: 30+ Drupal sites
  - Dartmouth College: homepage on Drupal
- Other notable universities: MIT, University of Oxford, Stanford, UCLA, University of Cambridge, Duke, University of Toronto, University of Arizona, George Washington University
- **Caveat:** Some of these institutions use Drupal for subsites/departments rather than their primary homepage. Princeton's homepage, for example, is not Drupal, though its graduate site is.
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.zyxware.com/articles/5478/all-ivy-league-colleges-use-drupal-for-their-websites
  - https://www.vardot.com/en-us/ideas/blog/drupal-higher-education-10-top-universities-why-they-choose-it

### Global Drupal Statistics (2025)

- **1.68 million** active websites powered by Drupal globally
- **1.5%** of all websites worldwide (but **12.8% of the world's top 10,000 websites**)
- **1.3 million** registered community members on drupal.org
- **135,206** Drupal sites in the United States alone
- Top countries: US (135K), Germany (21.5K), Russia (18.4K), France (18.4K), UK (15K)
- Enterprise penetration: 1.5% of Drupal users employ 10,001+ people (significantly higher than the <0.5% typical for other CMS platforms)
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.globalmediainsight.com/blog/drupal-statistics/
  - https://w3techs.com/technologies/details/cm-drupal
  - https://enlyft.com/tech/products/drupal
