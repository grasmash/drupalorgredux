## 1. Who We Are

Drupal and the open web go hand in hand. The open web is a web that is built for everyone -- a free, public internet that all people around the world can access for information, connection, commerce, and political participation.

Drupal, the world's leading open-source, community-built content management system, powers the open web with innovative, secure, accessible digital experiences. Both are driven by the same core principles: **access, freedom, inclusion, and empowerment**.

> Drupal has a soul. It is purpose over product. An open web for everyone.

### 1.1 Brand Attributes

The Drupal brand is defined by five core attributes:

- **Flexible**
- **Ambitious**
- **Open**
- **Powerful**
- **Secure**

### 1.2 Brand Voice

- **Welcoming and inclusive**: "Come for the code, stay for the community"
- **Community-focused**: Emphasizing open source, collaboration, and shared ownership
- **Empowering**: Highlighting flexibility, control, and developer/user agency
- **Professional but approachable**: Suitable for enterprise audiences while remaining accessible

### 1.3 Key Brand Messages

- "Create Remarkable Websites that Scale."
- "The Most Powerful Brands Run on Drupal."
- "Precision is in our code."
- "Freedom is in our code."
- "Creativity is in our code."
- "Drupal powers 1-in-40 websites."
- "In A World of Templates, Be An Original."
- "Fear No Monolith."
- "Born to Be Open."
- "Open Source. Endless Possibilities."
- "Come for the code, stay for the community."
- "Build the open web with Drupal."
- "Drupal helps make an Open Web possible."

## 2. Logo (The Drop)

### 2.1 The Drop

The Drupal Drop represents the fluid nature of the open web. It creates a ripple that extends outward, creating an impact that is greater than its size.

As Drupal has a legacy, the drop has evolved throughout Drupal's time on the web. Much like how the web is constantly evolving, the latest version is an evolution from previous versions. This evolution emphasizes the openness and community that Drupal could not exist without.

The drop is made up of several elements that represent the community:

- **The Greater Drop**: Represents classic Drupal and the impact it has had on the open web.
- **The Community Drop**: Represents many parts coming together to create a cohesive whole -- something new and beautiful.
- **The Individual Drop**: Drupal could not exist without individual contributions. It starts with a single person and the ultimate impact is on a single person. When you add it all together the impact is immeasurable.

### 2.2 Primary Logo

The primary logo consists of the Drupal Drop with the Drupal wordmark. It can be used in several combinations:

- **Stacked (Vertical)**: Drop icon above "Drupal" text
- **Horizontal**: Drop icon beside "Drupal" text

**Background usage:**
- **Dark backgrounds**: Use Drupal Blue or White versions.
- **Light backgrounds**: Use Drupal Navy.

The Drupal wordmark is created in **ZT Gatha**. Do not retype the wordmark to create the logo. Always work with an official file.

### 2.6 Logo Misuse

Do not misuse the logo in any way. Specific violations:

- **DON'T** change the size relationship between the Drupal Drop and the logotype.
- **DON'T** use any color besides the approved brand colors.
- **DON'T** outline the logo.
- **DON'T** add a drop shadow or glow effect to the logo.
- **DON'T** stretch or resize the logo in any way that is not proportional.
- **DON'T** rotate any part of the logo.
- **DON'T** re-key the Drupal wordmark or use any other font.
- **DON'T** use the logo on a busy background or image.
- **DON'T** change the opacity/transparency of the logo.
- **DON'T** use the full logo as a transparent window.
- **DON'T** integrate the Drupal logo into other logos or marks.
- **DON'T** use the logo to suggest official endorsement without permission.
- **DON'T** create derivative works without Drupal Association approval.

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Primary Font: ZT Gatha

A new typographic system has been created for Drupal. This system uses **ZT Gatha** for headlines, subheads, and H1 tags.

**Approved weights:**
- **Bold** (and Bold Italic)
- **Semi-Bold** (and Semi-Bold Italic)

When using a language that is not supported by ZT Gatha, please use **Noto Sans in Bold weight** as a fallback.

The Drupal wordmark is set in ZT Gatha. Do not retype the wordmark.

### 4.2 Secondary Font: Noto Sans

The secondary font is **Noto Sans**. This first "World" font has many options for other languages.

**Approved weights:**
- **Bold** and Bold Italic
- **Medium** and Medium Italic
- **Regular** and Italic

This font is used for the rest of the typographic system: **H2 tags, eyebrows, and body copy**.

### 4.3 Usage Hierarchy

| Element | Font | Weight |
|---------|------|--------|
| H1 / Headlines / Subheads | ZT Gatha | Bold or Semi-Bold |
| H2 tags | Noto Sans | Bold or Medium |
| Eyebrows | Noto Sans | Bold or Medium |
| Body copy | Noto Sans | Regular or Medium |
| Sub-branding | Noto Sans | Bold |
| Wordmark | ZT Gatha | Do not retype -- use official file |

### 4.4 Presentation Typography

In the official Drupal slide deck template, the following fonts are used:

- **Noto Sans Medium** -- headlines and emphasis
- **Noto Sans** -- body text (regular weight)
- **Noto Sans Light** -- lighter body text, secondary content
- **Helvetica Neue** -- alternate/accent font
- **Arial** -- bullet point characters

### 4.5 Fallback Fonts

- When ZT Gatha does not support a language: use **Noto Sans Bold**.
- Noto Sans supports many different languages with new characters being added all the time. Visit fonts.google.com for more options.

### 4.6 Downloadable Font Files

- ZT Gatha - Bold and SemiBold.zip

## 5. Graphic Elements

### 5.1 GUI Blocks

The Graphic User Interface has been crucial to bringing in the greatest amount of new users to computing. From the first operating systems to the most modern augmented reality systems, the GUI is still a vital part of computing.

Drupal's brand has incorporated this essential computing element to create a dynamic and user-friendly system.

**GUI Block anatomy:**
- Control Dots
- GUI Block (the main rectangle shape)
- Line Shadow

#### GUI Block Variations

The GUI Block can be created in a variety of:
- Color line combinations
- Color fills
- Image fills

**Rules:**
- Limit the number of colors to **3** in a single GUI Block.
- GUI Blocks do not have to always have the shadow lines or the control dots.

#### Creating GUI Blocks

1. Create a rectangle the size desired.
2. Round the corners.
3. Duplicate the rectangle and shift diagonally at a 45-degree angle. The shifted GUI Block should not be closer or further away than 3 line widths.
4. Round out inner and outer corners to match the radius of the corresponding corner.
5. Add control dots and shadow lines at 45 degrees.
6. Add between 0 to 3 control dots depending upon space available. Do not fill the control dots.

#### GUI Block Misuse

- **DON'T** keep mismatched corner radii (matching corners must have the same radius).
- **DON'T** use more than 3 colors (including white) in a single GUI Block.
- **DON'T** use gradients in a GUI Block.

#### GUI Block as Frame vs. Window

- **GUI Block as a frame**: When the focus is on one or two people, or if you need to see particular details of an image, or if you need to use an impactful headline.
- **GUI Block as a window**: When you need more space for copy or headlines, or have multiple images to deal with. Pair the background color or gradient with the theme of the headline or image.

#### Available GUI Block Files

**SVG:** Drupal GUI Block-01.svg through Drupal GUI Block-05.svg
**PNG:** Drupal GUI Block-01.png through Drupal GUI Block-05.png

### 5.2 Drop Window

The Drupal Drop can be used as a photography or videography window. Parts of the Drupal Drop can be sized up to create interesting window shapes for imagery. The drop shape acts as a clipping mask/window for photography.

**To create a Drop Window:**
1. Select a section of the Drupal Drop shape.
2. Size it up to create the desired window.
3. Use as a clipping mask for photography or video.

### 5.3 Dynamic Patterns

Several dynamic patterns have been created for usage in various designs. Inspired by classic computing screen savers and GUI elements, these patterns add depth to many designs.

**DO NOT** use unapproved patterns.

## 6. Photography

Photography is one of the most critical assets in a brand identity system.

### 6.1 Key Photography Attributes

The four key attributes of Drupal photos are:

1. **Warmth**
2. **Cooperation**
3. **Diversity**
4. **Friendliness**

### 6.2 Diversity & Inclusion

Photography should be representative of the diverse community that contributes to Drupal. Include people from a wide range of races, ethnicities, ages, gender expressions, sexual orientations, and abilities.

### 6.3 Photography Style

**DO:**
- Use natural light and realistic settings for the most authentic feeling photography.
- Lighting should always feel natural and appropriate to the setting.
- Colors should feel expressive and bright.

**DON'T:**
- Use black and white.
- Use muting, sepia, or duotone effects.
- Use artificially colored photography.
- Use HDR or any photography filters.

## 7. Sub-Branding

### 7.1 Typography

- **Noto Sans Bold** should be used for all sub-branding.

### 7.2 Colors

- Sub-brand colors allowed are **Drupal Blue** and **Drupal Navy**.
- If using a White logo, Drupal sub-branding should only be in White.

### 7.3 Rules

- When creating a sub-brand, download the appropriate, approved working file. Do not start from scratch.
- Noto Sans supports many different languages with new characters being added all the time. Visit fonts.google.com for more options.
- Sub-branding can be used in both stacked and horizontal layouts with the Drupal logo.
- Non-western character sets are supported (e.g., Japanese sub-branding).

## 8. Applications

### 8.1 Putting It All Together

It becomes easy and fun to put all brand elements together. A huge variety of representation can happen simply through following the guidelines.

**Design Decision Guide:**

The combinations can quickly become overwhelming. Consider:
- When to use the GUI Blocks as a frame on top of a full bleed image?
- When to use the GUI Block as a window for an image?
- When to use a gradient as a background?
- When to use a solid color as a background?

Always keep the **audience, imagery, and messaging length** in mind as you create modules, elements, ads, posters, and any other media.

A longer headline? Separate the image. Need more visual impact? Use the GUI Block as a frame.

### 8.2 Web Modules

Example modules for the web show how the new brand can be incorporated. These are for look and feel purposes only, not a technical grid layout system. See the web brand standards for more.

### 8.3 Presentations / Slides

Presentations can be tricky, but slide options are available to help create Drupal branded presentations. All typographic, GUI Block, image, and color rules apply.

**Slide Deck Template Details:**
- Format: 16:9 widescreen
- 35 slides with 26 custom layouts
- Primary background: Navy (#162C63)
- Fonts: Noto Sans family (Medium, Regular, Light), Helvetica Neue, Arial
- Rounded corner images are a consistent design motif
- White text on dark backgrounds is the standard pattern
- Multi-color accent blocks (lavender, coral, yellow, blue) appear on feature slides
- Background images should be 16:9 to avoid stretching

**Slide layout types available:**
- Title/Cover slides (dark and light variants)
- Brand statement slides
- Content layouts (A, B, C, D with various image configurations)
- Numbered content series (01-05) for multi-step content
- CTA/Tagline slides
- Quote/statement slides (coral and navy backgrounds)
- Image-focused layouts and multi-image galleries
- Data/statistics slides
- Closing/end slides

### 8.4 Swag / Merchandise

The same standards can be applied to physical swag. Patterns, color, and fonts all work well on merchandise. Examples include bucket hats, watch bands, and beanies featuring the Drupal drop logo with various pattern and color treatments.

### 8.5 Environmental / Trade Show

The same standards can be applied to 3D environments. Patterns, color, and fonts all work well in both 2D and 3D. Trade show applications include branded booth displays, vertical banners, and large 3D Drupal drop logo installations.

## 10. Trademark & Usage Rules

### 10.1 Ownership

- **"Drupal"** is a registered trademark of **Dries Buytaert**, who retains sole ownership.
- The **Drupal Association** holds copyrights for all brand materials, including logo variations.
- The **Druplicon** (the legacy community mascot) is licensed under GPL v2.

### 10.2 Required Attribution

Always include this attribution when using the Drupal trademark:

> *"Drupal is a registered trademark of Dries Buytaert."*

### 10.3 Automatic License (No Permission Needed)

You may use the Drupal trademark freely when:

- **Fostering Drupal software**: Extending, improving, or promoting Drupal adoption
- **Domain names/titles**: Including your own company name alongside "Drupal" (e.g., "Acme's Drupal Gold Course")
- **Books, videos, and media**: Instructional materials without suggesting official endorsement
- **Camps and meetups**: "DrupalCamp [Location]" or "Drupal Bootcamp [Year]" (cannot use "conference", "convention", or "association")
- **Unaltered logo usage**: Official Drupal logo in standalone, unmodified form
- **Nominative fair use**: Describing, reviewing, or discussing Drupal in text

### 10.4 License Grant Required (Apply Formally)

You must apply for permission when:

- Suggesting official connection (e.g., "drupalofficialfaq.com")
- Using "conference", "convention", or "association" terminology
- Generic domain names (e.g., "drupal-hosting.com")
- Registering Drupal as part of another trademark
- Altering the logo (modified colors, fonts, or combined logos)
- Company/organization names containing "Drupal"

**Apply to**: trademark@association.drupal.org

### 10.5 Prohibited Uses

- Creating derivative works without explicit approval
- Promoting non-affiliated products/services with Drupal branding
- Using brand materials for non-free or closed-source software promotion
- Illegal, defamatory, or humiliating purposes
- Altering or defacing brand materials
